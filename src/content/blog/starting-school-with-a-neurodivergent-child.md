Short answer: the right to support in Swedish school applies from the very first compulsory year, förskoleklass, not from the point where a child is visibly struggling. The same Skollagen chain that governs the rest of school, ledning och stimulans, then extra anpassningar, then särskilt stöd, applies in förskoleklass too, and none of it requires a diagnosis. For a neurodivergent child, the start of school is the single best moment to put support in place, because it is the point before small difficulties have had time to compound.

## Förskoleklass is where school, and the duty to support, begins

Since the autumn term of 2018, förskoleklass has been compulsory. Every child who turns six during the calendar year and lives in Sweden has skolplikt and starts in förskoleklass (skollagen, 7 kap.). That matters for support, because the school's duty to adapt teaching to each child does not switch on later in grundskolan. It applies from this first year.

The provisions on extra anpassningar, on investigating whether a child needs särskilt stöd, and on åtgärdsprogram all apply in förskoleklass (skollagen, 3 kap.). A six-year-old who needs a clearer structure, shorter steps, or a calmer way into the school day has the same legal right to get it as a student in year nine.

## Support does not wait for a diagnosis

The most common reason a neurodivergent child starts school without support is the belief that nothing can happen until there is an assessment. That is not what the law says. A child's right to extra anpassningar and särskilt stöd is triggered by [need, not by a diagnosis](/blog/support-without-a-diagnosis-school). A förskoleklass that waits for a neuropsychiatric evaluation, which in much of Sweden means [a long queue](/blog/neuropsychiatric-assessment-waiting-times), is not meeting its duty. It is postponing it.

This is also where Sweden's own early-support promise sits. The reading, writing and maths guarantee (läsa, skriva, räkna-garantin) starts in förskoleklass precisely so that difficulties are caught early, though [the guarantee is not always delivered in practice](/blog/swedens-early-support-guarantee-not-delivering). The intent of the law is unambiguous: the first year is for catching needs, not for waiting them out.

## What the chain looks like in the first year

The structure is the same one that runs through all of school. It starts with [ledning och stimulans](/blog/what-is-ledning-och-stimulans), teaching designed so more children can take part without anything extra. If that is not enough, the school adds [extra anpassningar](/blog/what-do-extra-anpassningar-look-like), concrete adjustments within ordinary teaching: visual schedules, shorter instructions, a predictable routine, a quiet place to land. If those are not enough, the school must investigate and, where the need is there, decide on [särskilt stöd and write an åtgärdsprogram](/blog/what-skollagen-requires-extra-anpassningar-sarskilt-stod). For a full picture of how the whole journey fits together, see [your child's right to support in Swedish school](/blog/your-childs-right-to-support-in-swedish-school).

## The handover from förskolan should not start from zero

If a child attended förskola, the people there often already know a great deal: what helps this child settle, what overwhelms them, which routines work. A good start to school carries that knowledge forward rather than making the förskoleklass rediscover it by trial and error. The överlämning from förskola to förskoleklass is one of the transitions where, with guardian consent, [information about a child's needs is meant to follow them](/blog/support-follows-student-changing-schools). When it does, the first weeks are calmer for everyone.

## Why the first year matters most

Nuro exists because the cost of getting this wrong is not paid at the start. It is paid years later, when unmet needs have hardened into falling behind, into anxiety about school, and eventually into the absence a school then has a [duty to investigate](/blog/school-duty-to-investigate-absence). [School refusal rarely starts in the year it becomes visible](/blog/school-absence-starts-earlier); it starts with early experiences of a school that did not fit.

The start of school is the cheapest possible moment to make school fit. A clear picture of what a child needs, built from the first year and kept as a living record rather than reconstructed each time a teacher changes, is what lets support arrive early and stay consistent. For a neurodivergent six-year-old, that is the difference between a school they can belong to and one they slowly learn to dread.

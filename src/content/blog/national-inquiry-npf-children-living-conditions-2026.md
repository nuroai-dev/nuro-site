## The short answer

On 2 July 2026 the Swedish government decided the terms of reference for a new national inquiry: [Kommittédirektiv 2026:68, Goda och jämlika livsvillkor för barn och unga med neuropsykiatriska funktionsnedsättningar](https://www.riksdagen.se/sv/dokument-och-lagar/dokument/kommittedirektiv/goda-och-jamlika-livsvillkor-for-barn-och-unga-med_heb168/) (Good and equal living conditions for children and young people with NPF). The inquiry is tasked with analysing why neuropsychiatric diagnoses among children and young people are rising, particularly ADHD and autism, working out the consequences across healthcare, social services and schools, and proposing measures to give these children better and more equal life chances. It must report by 1 December 2027.

For schools, the useful thing to understand is what this does and does not change right now. It is a fact-finding and proposal remit, not a new law. Nothing about a school's current duties changes because the inquiry exists. But schools are named explicitly as one of the sectors whose situation it must examine, which is a signal about where the state now sees part of the answer.

## What the inquiry is actually asked to do

The directive sets the inquiry a chain of tasks. In the government's own words, it must "analysera och beskriva bakgrunden till ökningen av neuropsykiatriska diagnoser bland barn och unga, särskilt när det gäller adhd och autism" (analyse and describe the background to the increase in neuropsychiatric diagnoses among children and young people, particularly regarding ADHD and autism). From there it is to identify the factors driving that increase, analyse the consequences for healthcare, social services, schools and other parts of society, and "lämna förslag på åtgärder för att stärka och utveckla samhällets förmåga att bidra till goda och jämlika livsvillkor" (submit proposals for measures to strengthen and develop society's capacity to contribute to good and equal living conditions). Where needed, it is to bring legislative proposals.

The framing matters. This is not an inquiry into whether children are "over-diagnosed" or a debate about labels. It starts from the fact of a rising number and asks a practical question: given more children with these conditions, what does each part of society, schools included, need to be able to do.

## Why the rise is real, and why school is in the frame

The rise the inquiry is responding to is well documented. Socialstyrelsen has reported that the share of children and young people with an ADHD diagnosis rose by [up to 50 percent between 2019 and 2022, with the sharpest increase among girls](/blog/adhd-diagnoses-rising-fastest-among-girls). More children with ADHD and autism in the population is, mechanically, more children with these conditions in every classroom.

And school is where the consequences of getting support wrong show up first and most measurably. Parliament's own follow-up found that only [62.4 percent of students with NPF reach eligibility for a vocational upper-secondary programme, against 87.2 percent of their peers](/blog/swedens-parliament-npf-school-results). When support arrives late or unevenly, the gap widens, and it widens most for the children who were [identified late in the first place](/blog/girls-with-npf-receive-less-sarskilt-stod). An inquiry that treats rising numbers as a whole-of-society capacity question, rather than an individual medical one, is looking at the same problem this blog keeps returning to from the school side.

## What it means for schools between now and 2027

The honest read for a school leader or teacher is that the inquiry changes nothing about today. A report due in December 2027, followed by any legislation, is years from the classroom. Meanwhile the duty is unchanged: a school must still give a struggling student [extra anpassningar and, where needed, särskilt stöd](/blog/what-skollagen-requires-extra-anpassningar-sarskilt-stod), decided by need, starting now. The children the inquiry is about are in school this term.

That is the gap between a policy timeline and a child's timeline, and it is [the gap Nuro is built to close](/blog/the-right-exists-the-system-doesnt). Whatever the inquiry proposes in 2027, the work in front of schools now is the same: seeing which student needs which adaptation, in time, and delivering it without asking one teacher to build thirty versions of every lesson by hand. A rising number of neurodivergent students makes that capacity problem more urgent, not less, and it is the part we build for.
